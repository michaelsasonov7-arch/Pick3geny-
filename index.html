❓ How To Use Pick3Geny
🚀 Quick Start — Step by Step

Step 1 — Select Methods: Choose which of the 4 algorithms you want to run. All 4 is recommended. Method 60 + X-Circle is the best pair.

Step 2 — Enter Your Draws: In the Draw inputs, enter your 6 most recent lottery results. Draw 1 = most recent, Draw 2 = one before that, etc. Numbers must be exactly 3 digits.

Step 3 — Enter Actual Result (optional but recommended): If you know today's winning number, enter it in "Actual Draw Result" BEFORE clicking Run. This lets the app track your hits automatically.

Step 4 — Click RUN ANALYSIS: The app runs all selected algorithms and builds your Money Table.

Step 5 — Check the Money Table: Focus on 🟡 yellow (2-method) and 🟢 green (3-method) numbers. These have the strongest statistical consensus.

Step 6 — Play your numbers: Click any normalized number to see all its playable variations. Example: 123 → 123, 132, 213, 231, 312, 321.

🔢 What are normalized numbers and why?

Pick 3 numbers are "normalized" by sorting their digits in ascending order. This means all arrangements of the same digits become one entry:

  • 321, 312, 213, 231, 132, 123 all become 123
  • 223, 232, 322 all become 223
  • 666 stays 666 (only one variation)

There are only ~220 unique normalized 3-digit numbers (vs 1,000 total). This is why you must play ALL variations of each number you choose. Click any number in the Money Table to see its full list of variations.

🎨 Color System — What each color means

🔴 Red — 1 Method (Weak): Only one algorithm found this number. Low confidence. These are the most numerous but least reliable numbers in your table.

🟡 Yellow — 2 Methods (STRONG ⭐): Two different algorithms independently produced this number. This is the "sweet spot" — good consensus without being noise. These are your best plays.

🟢 Green — 3 Methods (STRONG ⭐): Three algorithms agree. Very strong consensus. High confidence numbers. Play these first.

⚪ White/Gold — 4 Methods (Very Weak): All 4 algorithms agree — but this is actually a bad sign. When every algorithm produces the same number, it's usually because it's a statistically common or trivial result. These numbers are often "noise" and should be the last ones you consider.

Bottom line: Yellow + Green = your core plays. Red = secondary. White = almost ignore.

📥 How to enter draws correctly

The 6 input rows represent your 6 most recent lottery draws, in reverse chronological order:

  • Draw 1: The most recent lottery result (yesterday's or today's)
  • Draw 2: The result before Draw 1
  • Draw 3–6: Continue going back in time

Why this order matters: Method 60 and Magic Pair are sensitive to the relationship between consecutive draws. Correct ordering gives more accurate results.

Not all rows required: Method 123 and X-Circle only need Draw 1. Method 60 needs Draws 1–2. Magic Pair needs all 6. The app adjusts input rows automatically based on selected methods.

⬇️ Shift Down, Random Fill, and Quick Fill

⬇️ Shift Down: Moves every row down by one position (Draw 1 → Draw 2, Draw 2 → Draw 3, etc.). Draw 6 is deleted, Draw 1 becomes empty. Use this after a new lottery draw: first Shift Down, then type the new draw into Draw 1. This is the fastest workflow for regular users.

🎲 Random Fill: Fills ONLY empty rows with random 3-digit numbers. Already filled rows are not touched. Perfect after Shift Down — it fills just the empty Draw 1 slot.

📋 Quick Fill: Enter multiple numbers separated by spaces in one text box, then click Fill. Example: type "123 456 789 012 345 678" to fill all 6 rows at once.

📊 Understanding the Statistics

Hit Rate: The percentage of runs where the actual winning number appeared in your Money Table. A 50% hit rate means 1 in 2 runs contained the winning number.

Avg Between Hits: How many runs (on average) pass between successful predictions. Lower is better. Using all 4 methods averages ~1.98 runs between hits.

Overlap Numbers: Numbers that appear in 2+ methods simultaneously. These are your most reliable indicators. The more runs with overlaps, the stronger your system is performing.

Strength Score: A cumulative score per number based on how many methods found it, across all runs. A number with score 10 appeared in multiple methods across multiple runs — these are your "core" numbers to watch.

Pre-Hit Numbers: Numbers that appeared in Money Tables 1–3 runs before a hit occurred. These may act as "warning signals" — if you see them appearing now, a hit may be coming soon.

💾 History and Storage — Where is my data?

All your run history is stored in your browser's localStorage — a private database on your device. Key facts:

  • ✅ Data never leaves your device. No server, no cloud, no account required.
  • ✅ Data persists when you close and reopen the app.
  • ✅ Your 6 input numbers are also saved and restored automatically.
  • ⚠️ If you click "Clear All History", data is permanently deleted from localStorage on your device. This cannot be undone.
  • ⚠️ If you clear your browser data/cookies, your history may also be deleted.
  • 💡 Use Export JSON to create a backup file before clearing. You can import it later by contacting the developer for a future import feature.

Important — localStorage is per browser, per device:

  • Your history in Chrome browser is completely separate from your history in Mi Browser, Safari, Firefox, or any other browser — even on the same phone.
  • This is how browsers work: each browser has its own private localStorage storage area. They do NOT share data with each other.
  • Solution: Always use the same browser for Pick3Geny. If you want to move your data from Mi Browser to Chrome (or vice versa), use Export JSON in the History tab and then Import JSON in the other browser.

Note: localStorage is also separate per device. Your phone and your computer have separate histories even in the same browser.

🔥 Trend Button — What it does

The 🔥 Trend button is in the Money Table toolbar, next to the sort buttons. It shows you which numbers are currently HOT or COLD based on the last 10 runs.

  • 🔥 Fire icon = the number appeared 6 or more times in the last 10 runs. This is a HOT, consistent number.
  • ~ Tilde = appeared 3–5 times. Neutral momentum.
  • ❄️ Snowflake = appeared 0–2 times recently. This number is COLD — it's fading or statistically due to return.

How to use: Click Trend once to turn ON (button highlights). Each number shows its icon + count below it. Click again to turn OFF and return to normal view.

Why it matters: Overlap tells you if multiple algorithms agree. Trend tells you if the number has been consistently appearing lately. A number that is both 🟢 Green (3-method overlap) AND 🔥 Hot is your strongest possible play.

✅ AI Smart Pick — What it does and how to use it

The ✅ AI Smart Pick button is in the Money Table toolbar. It ranks every number in your current Money Table using 5 scoring models and shows you the Top 10 picks in a card above the RUN button.

The 5 models:

  • (1) Overlap strength — numbers found by 2–3 methods score highest (best consensus).
  • (2) Hot momentum — numbers that appeared frequently in the last 10 runs get a score boost.
  • (3) Breakout detector — numbers not seen recently but statistically overdue get extra points.
  • (4) Near-miss learning — numbers that share 2–3 digits with your recent real draw results score higher.
  • (5) Method accuracy — each method is weighted by its actual hit rate in your personal history.

How to use:

  • Step 1 — Run analysis to get a Money Table.
  • Step 2 — Click ✅ AI Smart Pick.
  • Step 3 — See the Top 10 picks ranked by combined score. 🥇🥈🥉 = top 3.
  • Step 4 — Click any number to see all its playable variations.
  • Step 5 — Track AI performance over time in Stats → ✅ AI Picks.

Reading the scores: Higher score = stronger signal from multiple models. The AI improves over time as it learns your lottery patterns. After 10+ uses, the AI Pick Rate in Stats becomes a meaningful performance indicator.

Important: This is a statistical ranking tool, not a lottery prediction system. No method can guarantee wins. Use it as one additional signal alongside the Money Table.

🔄 Refresh Stats — What it does

The 🔄 Refresh Stats button is in the Stats tab header.

What it does: Recomputes all 13 statistics sections from scratch using your complete run history. This includes hit rates, overlap distributions, digit frequencies, sequences, AI performance, and the summary.

When to use it:

  • If you switched to the Stats tab from another tab and the data looks outdated.
  • After importing a JSON backup — the stats don't auto-refresh on import.
  • If you notice the numbers don't match your History tab count.

Do you need it often? Usually no. Stats auto-refresh every time you run analysis or delete a run while the Stats tab is already open. Refresh is a manual override for edge cases.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why does the same input give different results with different methods?
A: Each of the 4 algorithms uses a completely different mathematical approach. Method 123 uses modular addition patterns. Method 60 uses block calculations with forward/reverse operations. X-Circle builds a 3×3 grid. Magic Pair analyzes digit pairs across rows. They're designed to complement each other.

Q: Can I use only 1 or 2 methods?
A: Yes. Uncheck any methods you don't want. The input rows will adjust automatically. Using fewer methods produces fewer numbers in your Money Table — which may be easier to play but may lower your hit rate.

Q: Why does 4-method (white) indicate weakness?
A: When all 4 completely different algorithms independently produce the same number, it suggests that number is statistically common or trivially predictable — not a strong prediction signal. The strongest signal is 2–3 algorithms independently converging on the same number.

Q: What is ABC, AAB, AAA?
A: These describe the pattern of digits. ABC = all three digits different (e.g. 123). AAB = one digit repeated (e.g. 112, 122, 211). AAA = all three digits the same (e.g. 111, 222). Statistically, ABC numbers cover ~72% of all normalized combinations.

Q: How accurate is the 1.98 average?
A: This figure is based on historical analysis of the algorithms using real lottery data. It means that when using all 4 methods together, on average, the winning number appears in your Money Table approximately once every 2 runs. This is a statistical average — individual sessions may vary significantly.

Q: Is this a gambling app?
A: No. Pick3Geny is a statistical analysis tool. It generates number combinations based on mathematical algorithms applied to past lottery results. Whether or not to gamble — and how much — is entirely your own decision and responsibility.
